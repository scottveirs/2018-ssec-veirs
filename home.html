SRKW do respond to noise

2003: U.S. Navy uses mid-frequency sonar in Haro Strait with SRKWs at surface in front of Center for Whale Research 2009: SRKWs "speak up" -- +1 dB in S1 call source level for +1 dB in broadband noise from nearby boats (>105 dB?) (Holt, Noren, Veirs, Emmons & Veirs)

But anecdotally, they also seem to be able to adapt to it:
  • SRKWs ride the wakes of high-speed vessel (often close to propellers)
  • They frequent the Salish Sea which is relatively noisy compared to the outer coast of WA/BC
  • Source levels of signals they emit are MUCH higher than almost everything they receive

  1. Ships dominate the Haro Strait soundscape
